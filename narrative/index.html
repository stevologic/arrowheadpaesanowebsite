The X’s & O’s

Concepts drawn up on the board — what it is, why it works, and the coaching point.

Offense

Under-center play-action boot

Situation 1st & 10, early down — knee-management down

Sells the run, moves the launch point off the rush, and gives Mahomes a clean half-field read without asking the knee to climb a muddy pocket.

Coaching point Marry the boot to the actual inside-zone look you're running so the linebackers have to honor the fake.

Offense

RB screen / easy offense

Situation 2nd & long or vs. heavy pressure

Turns an aggressive rush into a negative for the defense and gets the ball out of Mahomes' hands before the knee is ever tested.

Coaching point Sell max-protect for a beat, then release the linemen — timing is everything on the slip.

Spagnuolo simulated pressure

Situation 3rd & 5+, obvious passing down

Shows heat, drops a lineman into a hook, and rushes the second level to confuse protection while the coverage stays sound behind it.

Coaching point Disguise is the point: the picture pre-snap must look like man pressure even when it's zone behind it.

The story so far

Always looking ahead

Chiefs camp opened at Missouri Western State University, St. Joseph, MO with one question under everything: how ready is Patrick Mahomes's knee — and how much can a retooled identity carry until it is?

The story of this camp is timing. Patrick Mahomes is rebuilding from a torn ACL/LCL suffered last December, and every rep in St. Joseph is a referendum on how close he is to full strength for Week 1. Andy Reid has treated the ramp with caution, because the moment Mahomes practices in earnest, the PUP contingency comes off the table.

On offense, Eric Bieniemy is back as coordinator with a clear mandate: harder edges, cleaner fundamentals, and an identity that doesn't ask Mahomes to be the answer on every third-and-long. Expect more under-center looks, play-action off a real run threat, and 'easy offense' — screens and spacing — to protect a knee that's still getting its legs.

On defense, Steve Spagnuolo is again the steadying hand. The camp battles that matter most are on the back end: who wins the outside corner job and, especially, the slot. If the young secondary holds up, the pass rush can tee off and this defense can carry the early weeks while the offense finds its timing.

Everything points forward to Week 1 · Tue Sep 15 vs Denver Broncos. For context, the model gives KC 59.4%; Vegas has it KC -3; Polymarket prices the Chiefs at 5.9% to win it all. The question every practice answers a little more: is Kansas City ready to flip the 2025 script?

The storylines

What we’re watching

QB1

Mahomes' rehab ramp vs. PUP math

The Chiefs can't treat camp like a normal build-up: once Mahomes practices fully, he can't be placed on PUP. Watch how they meter his team reps and whether he sees any preseason snaps.

Scheme

Bieniemy-ball: identity over hero-ball

More under center, more play-action, fewer 3rd-and-forever downs. The install is about winning early downs so the offense functions even on a managed snap count.

Trenches

Right tackle & the protection plan

The line's job is to keep a rehabbing QB clean. The open tackle competition and the interior's veteran core set the ceiling for how aggressive KC can be down the field.

Secondary

The slot corner is the swing job

In Spagnuolo's defense the nickel is a starter. Whoever wins the slot determines how much disguise and pressure KC can throw at opposing quarterbacks.

Rookies

Which draft pick has to play now

Premium picks were spent on defense. The Chiefs need at least one rookie contributing immediately to look like themselves again.

Backfield

A real run game as a pressure valve

An early-down run threat is what makes play-action honest and keeps the offense on schedule while Mahomes ramps up.
